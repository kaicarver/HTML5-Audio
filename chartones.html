This page has various attempts to display tones for Chinese characters. Zoom in with Ctrl-+ to see details.

Something similar could be used for pinyin, whose tone marks are always too small. If tones are important, then their visibility should be as high as the rest of the pinyin notation. Otherwise tones remain a second-class aspect.

This solution with tones in the character background using a span of a different class for each tone works ok:

媽麻馬罵嗎
媽麻馬罵嗎
媽麻馬罵嗎
媽麻馬罵嗎
媽麻馬罵嗎

Tone above could look ok too (not yet fully done):

漢漢漢漢漢
漢漢漢漢漢
媽麻馬罵嗎

One problem with using this for pinyin is you probably don't want the tone mark proportional to the size of the pinyin syllable:

ma ma ma ma ma
miao miao miao miao miao
a a a a a
